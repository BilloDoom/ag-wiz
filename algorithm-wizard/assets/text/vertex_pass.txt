[font_size=16][b]Overview[/b][/font_size]
The vertex pass is the [b]first stage[/b] of the rendering pipeline. During this stage, the GPU processes each vertex of every mesh in the scene.

[font_size=16][b]What Happens[/b][/font_size]
[color=#88ff88]1.[/color] [b]Vertex Fetching[/b]
   The GPU reads vertex data from buffers (position, normal, UV, etc.)

[color=#88ff88]2.[/color] [b]Vertex Shader Execution[/b]
   Each vertex is transformed through several coordinate spaces:
   [code]Local Space → World Space → View Space → Clip Space[/code]

[color=#88ff88]3.[/color] [b]Matrix Transformations[/b]
   • [color=#ffaa55]Model Matrix[/color] - Transforms from local to world space
   • [color=#ffaa55]View Matrix[/color] - Transforms from world to camera space
   • [color=#ffaa55]Projection Matrix[/color] - Applies perspective/orthographic projection

[font_size=16][b]Wireframe Visualization[/b][/font_size]
The wireframe view shows the [b]edge connectivity[/b] between vertices, revealing:
• Mesh topology and structure
• Triangle density and distribution
• How complex geometry is constructed
