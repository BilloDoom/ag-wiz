[font_size=16][b]Overview[/b][/font_size]
The post-processing pass applies [b]lighting calculations[/b] and visual effects to the rasterized image. This is where the scene comes to life with shadows, reflections, and atmospheric effects.

[font_size=16][b]What Happens[/b][/font_size]
[color=#ff88ff]1.[/color] [b]Lighting Calculations[/b]
   For each fragment, the GPU calculates light contributions:
   • [color=#ffaa55]Direct Lighting[/color] - Light directly hitting surfaces
   • [color=#ffaa55]Shadows[/color] - Areas occluded from light sources
   • [color=#ffaa55]Ambient[/color] - Indirect environmental lighting

[color=#ff88ff]2.[/color] [b]Advanced Lighting[/b]
   Modern renderers add sophisticated effects:
   • [color=#88aaff]SSAO[/color] - Screen-space ambient occlusion
   • [color=#88aaff]SSR[/color] - Screen-space reflections
   • [color=#88aaff]SDFGI/GI[/color] - Global illumination

[color=#ff88ff]3.[/color] [b]Post Effects[/b]
   Final image adjustments:
   • [color=#88ff88]Tonemapping[/color] - HDR to LDR conversion
   • [color=#88ff88]Bloom/Glow[/color] - Bright area bleeding
   • [color=#88ff88]Color Grading[/color] - Artistic color adjustments
   • [color=#88ff88]Anti-Aliasing[/color] - Edge smoothing (TAA/FXAA)
